How to
control it ?

controlling climate change is essential for safeguarding the environment, protecting human health and well-being, ensuring food and water security, promoting economic stability and social justice, and fostering global stability and peace

1.

Transition to
Renewable
Energy

Prioritize phasing out fossil fuels and accelerating the transition to renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydropower to significantly reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

2.

Protecting and
Restoring
Forests

3.

Carbon
Pricing

Establish carbon pricing mechanisms such as carbon taxes or cap-and-trade systems to create economic incentives for businesses to reduce emissions and invest in cleaner technologies.

4.

Energy
Efficiency

Implement policies and incentives to improve energy efficiency across industries, transportation, buildings, and appliances, reducing energy consumption and emissions.

5.

International Cooperation
and Agreements

By actively participating in such agreements, India commits to collectively addressing climate change alongside other countries. Additionally, as a developing nation with significant emissions, India benefits from the support provided by such agreements, including technology transfer and financial assistance

Combatting
Climate Change

Transition to renewables, boost energy efficiency, protect forests, enact carbon pricing, and collaborate internationally. Together, we cut emissions, conserve ecosystems, and advance sustainability for a resilient future.
